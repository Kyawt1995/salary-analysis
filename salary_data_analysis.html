In[14]:
mean_salary_by_title = salaries_df.groupby('job_title')['salary_in_usd'].mean()
max_salary_by_title = salaries_df.groupby('job_title')['salary_in_usd'].max()
min_salary_by_title = salaries_df.groupby('job_title')['salary_in_usd'].min()
count_by_title = salaries_df.groupby('job_title').size()

df_by_title = pd.DataFrame(mean_salary_by_title).reset_index()
df_by_title.columns = ['Job Title', 'Mean Salary']

df_by_title['Max Salary'] = df_by_title['Job Title'].map(max_salary_by_title)
df_by_title['Min Salary'] = df_by_title['Job Title'].map(min_salary_by_title)
df_by_title['Count'] = df_by_title['Job Title'].map(count_by_title)

df_by_title = round(df_by_title.sort_values(by='Mean Salary', ascending=False),2)

Mean Salary Distribution by Remote Ratio

In[17]:
mean_by_remote_ratio = salaries_df.groupby("remote_ratio")["salary_in_usd"].mean()

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
sns.boxplot(x="remote_ratio", y="salary_in_usd", data=salaries_df, showmeans=True)

plt.title('Mean Salary Distribution by Remote Ratio')
plt.xlabel('Remote Ratio')
plt.ylabel('Mean Salary (USD)')

plt.show()
Although there may be outliers present, our analysis highlights a clear trend: mean salaries are highest in fully remote and non-remote work environments, with a notable decrease is observed in partially remote settings. This shows that policies regarding remote work and pay scales are correlated, highlighting the strategic significance of remote work arrangements in determining pay scales.

Mean Salary Distribution by Company Location

In[19]:
mean_by_location = round(salaries_df.groupby("company_location")["salary_in_usd"].mean(), 2)
sorted_location = sorted(mean_by_location.items(), key=lambda kv: kv[1], reverse=True)
location_df = pd.DataFrame(sorted_location, columns=["company_location", "mean_salary"])

plt.figure(figsize=(20, 10))
sns.barplot(data=location_df, x="company_location", y="mean_salary",linewidth=2)
plt.xticks(rotation=90)
plt.xlabel("Company Location", size = 12)
plt.ylabel("Mean Salary (USD)", size =12)
plt.title("Mean Salary Distribution by Company Location", size = 20)
plt.show()
The difference in average salaries between different nations highlights the diversity of the world's salary markets and economic environments. Qatar has the highest average salary by far, whereas Israel, Puerto Rico, and the United States have greater average incomes, which may indicate strong economies and booming businesses. However, the average salary in nations like Ecuador, Moldova, and Honduras is lower, which may indicate different cost of living considerations or possible economic difficulties. The significance of comprehending regional economic dynamics and their influence on compensation levels is highlighted by this discrepancy.
